\section{Experimental Results and Comparison}

\subsection{Our method}

We first examine our short-time FFT template method. Since the task is single-label four-class classification, exact-match accuracy is the most direct metric, while label-wise accuracy is only a supplementary reference.

This method no longer uses only four representative case recordings as templates. It first averages the maximum-energy blocks of same-class samples in the training set and then re-estimates the final templates from the training and development sets after the block length is selected. The block length is chosen from 3 to 6 frames on the development set, and the selected value is 5. Because the data cover 300 words, cross-word and speaker variation are large, so FFT magnitude correlation alone cannot fully separate the four initial consonants. Even so, the method keeps a clear spectral interpretation.

\subsection{AI reimplementation comparison}

Beyond our method, the report also includes an AI reimplementation. It does not simply replace the classifier. It redoes the first two stages of the pipeline: it first uses log-mel features and energy change to obtain a more stable local spectrum near the word onset, and then uses positive-negative template differences and autocorrelation for detection.

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \small
    \setlength{\tabcolsep}{4pt}
    \caption{Overall comparison between our method and the AI reimplementation.}
    \label{tab:course-compare}
    \begin{tabular}{lccccc}
        \toprule
        Method & Label acc. & Exact match & Macro-F1 & Micro-F1 & Samples-F1 \\
        \midrule
        Our method & 0.6327 & 0.2655 & 0.2439 & 0.2655 & 0.2655 \\
        AI reimplementation & 0.7513 & 0.5026 & 0.4974 & 0.5026 & 0.5026 \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \caption{Per-class F1 of our method and the AI reimplementation.}
    \label{tab:course-per-label}
    \begin{tabular}{lcccc}
        \toprule
        Method & /g/ & /b/ & /d/ & /z/ \\
        \midrule
        Our method & 0.217 & 0.163 & 0.243 & 0.353 \\
        AI reimplementation & 0.464 & 0.359 & 0.429 & 0.737 \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The gain of the AI reimplementation mainly comes from two changes. It narrows the search range more precisely to the region near the word onset, and it adds explicit positive-negative template contrast inside the local window. The largest gain appears on /z/, which suggests that sustained frication is more stable under local log-mel templates and autocorrelation. The overlap between /b/ and /d/ remains strong, which means that short stop releases and alignment error still weaken the template evidence.

The classes /g/, /b/, and /d/ still overlap strongly, which explains why the AI reimplementation improves the results but does not remove confusion among stop consonants.
