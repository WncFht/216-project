\section{Conclusion}

This report studies spectral description and detection for the initial voiced consonants /g/, /b/, /d/, and /z/. After filtering the data by actual pronunciation, the dataset contains 75 words per class, for a total of 300 words and 6796 utterances. This definition gives a clearer label boundary than a filter based on spelling alone.

On this dataset, our method forms a complete detection pipeline by using only short-time FFT magnitude spectra and normalized correlation. The best block length selected on the development set is 5 frames, and the resulting test exact-match accuracy is 0.265. The AI reimplementation raises exact-match accuracy to 0.503 by adding a local log-mel onset window, positive-negative template contrast, and autocorrelation. The MLP reaches 0.485 exact-match accuracy, and the CNN reaches 0.559. In overall score, the CNN performs best, but the template methods provide a clearer view of the acoustic evidence. Across all methods, /z/ is the easiest class, while the short stop releases of /g/, /b/, and /d/ remain the main source of error because they depend more strongly on alignment.
