\section{Discussion and Limitations}

The reconstructed task separates three roles. The strict FFT template method demonstrates what the course-level tools can explain. The extended template method measures how much accuracy improves after adding onset localization, contrastive templates, and autocorrelation. The MLP and CNN provide learned baselines for comparison.

The main limitation is that the input remains a whole-word recording rather than a manually segmented phone. The strict method therefore relies on sliding-window search, and short stop bursts can be missed when the chosen block is slightly misaligned. This affects /g/ and /d/ most strongly. /z/ benefits from longer frication, so it gives the cleanest score separation under both template methods.

These results argue for a layered evaluation. A higher score alone does not explain which acoustic cue the method used. The strict template method gives the clearest view of the local spectral evidence; the extended method improves recognition by adding localization and contrastive statistics; the learned baselines show that more flexible models are not automatically better on a small, onset-sensitive corpus.
