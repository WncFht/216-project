## Setting

- Dataset: Mini LibriSpeech `dev-clean-2` + `train-clean-5`.
- Utterances used: 1800 sentence-level recordings.
- Speakers: 54.
- Split: speaker-independent, seed 216.
- Train: 1358 utterances, 40 speakers.
- Test: 442 utterances, 14 speakers.
- Labels: utterance-level voiced-consonant presence generated from transcripts by CMUdict/ARPABET.
- Device: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4070 SUPER, CUDA, PyTorch 2.11.0+cu130.

## Interpretation

Compared with the word-level Speech Commands v0.02 main experiment, Mini
LibriSpeech is much more diverse and uses longer sentence audio.  Course
FFT/STFT methods become stronger because the labels are utterance-level
presence labels: long sentences contain many voiced consonants, so broad
spectral summaries can often identify likely presence.  This is useful for a
supplementary comparison, but it is not a substitute for phone-level
segmentation.

The hard labels are `/jh/`, `/y/`, and especially `/zh/`, mainly because support
is low or acoustic evidence is brief relative to a 6-second sentence window.
