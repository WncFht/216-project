\begin{abstract}
This report studies the VE216 Project Topic 2 speech analysis task and focuses on the initial voiced consonants /g/, /b/, /d/, and /z/. The data come from MSWC English. We keep only words whose first letter is \texttt{g/b/d/z} and whose first non-vowel phone is the matching \texttt{G/B/D/Z}. Because the /z/ class contains only 75 eligible words, the final dataset uses 75 words per class, for a total of 300 words and 6796 utterances. We first present a detection method based on short-time FFT magnitude spectra and normalized correlation. We then present an AI reimplementation that uses log-mel features, onset localization, positive-negative templates, and autocorrelation. Finally, we compare both template methods with an MLP and a CNN. On the test set, exact-match accuracy is 0.265 for our method, 0.503 for the AI reimplementation, 0.485 for the MLP, and 0.559 for the CNN.
\end{abstract}
